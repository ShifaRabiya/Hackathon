Flavours of India

BIRIYANI

Biriyani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish, widely loved across India and beyond. It is made with basmati rice, tender pieces of marinated meat (usually chicken, mutton, or beef), and a blend of aromatic spices like cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, and saffron. The dish is cooked using the "dum" method, where rice and meat are steamed together to blend the rich flavors. It is often served with a side of raita (yogurt salad), pickle, and sometimes boiled eggs. Whether in its Hyderabadi, Kolkata, or Malabar variations, Biriyani is a rich, festive meal that embodies the essence of Indian spices and culinary traditions.

DOSA & SAMBAR

Dosa is a thin, crispy pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black lentils). It is a quintessential dish from South India, enjoyed for breakfast or dinner. The dosa is typically served with a variety of chutneys, like coconut and tomato chutneys, and most famously paired with Sambar, a spiced lentil soup made with tamarind, vegetables, and a mix of mustard seeds, curry leaves, and dry red chilies. The balance of textures, from the crispness of the dosa to the warmth and depth of the sambar, creates a delightful and satisfying meal.

BUTTER CHICKEN

Butter Chicken, also known as "Murgh Makhani," is a globally beloved Indian dish that originated in Delhi. The dish consists of succulent chicken pieces cooked in a rich and creamy tomato-based gravy, flavored with a blend of spices including garam masala, ginger, garlic, and fenugreek. The creamy butter sauce imparts a mellow yet rich taste, with just a hint of sweetness and smokiness. It is typically served with naan (flatbread) or steamed rice, making it a perfect comfort food for any occasion.

ONAM SADHYA

Onam Sadhya is a grand vegetarian feast traditionally served during the Onam festival in Kerala. This elaborate meal consists of a variety of curries, rice, and side dishes, all served on a banana leaf. The spread includes Sambar, Avial (a mixed vegetable curry with coconut), Kalan (yogurt-based curry), Thoran (stir-fried vegetables with coconut), Pachadi (yogurt-based salad), and Payasam (a sweet dessert made from rice, milk, and jaggery). The entire meal is carefully crafted to balance flavors of sweetness, tanginess, spice, and salt. It represents the abundant harvest and hospitality of Kerala, and every bite is a celebration of the state's culinary traditions.

PAV BHAJI

Pav Bhaji is a popular and flavorful street food dish from Maharashtra, India, loved across the country for its rich taste and comforting appeal. It consists of a spicy, mashed vegetable curry (bhaji) served with soft, butter-toasted bread rolls (pav). The bhaji is made by simmering a medley of vegetables such as potatoes, tomatoes, peas, and capsicum, cooked with aromatic spices and a generous amount of butter, giving it its signature taste and creamy texture. The dish is garnished with chopped onions, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lemon juice, adding freshness and tang.
